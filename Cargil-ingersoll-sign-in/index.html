VISITOR AGREEMENT, WAIVER AND RELEASE

As a condition of accessing the facilities ("Facility") of Cargill Meat Solutions Corporation, Cargill Kitchen Solutions, Inc., Cargill Turkey Production, LLC, Five Star Custom Foods, Ltd. and Cargill Limited (collectively "Cargill"), I agree to the following:

1. Safety: I will abide by all safety rules and other instructions or requests provided to me by Cargill.

2. Confidentiality: I will not disclose to others or use any Confidential Information disclosed or learned by me as a result of my visit to the Facility. "Confidential Information" shall mean any and all business, technical, and financial information, including by way of example but without limitation, products, formulations, specifications, manufacturing processes, samples, recipes, technical, commercial, economic and business affairs, and other information disclosed by Cargill either directly or indirectly or otherwise learned or observed while visiting a Cargill Facility. All rights in Confidential Information, including but not limited to any intellectual property rights, and all rights in the material containing Confidential Information provided by Cargill is and shall remain the property of Cargill or its affiliates. Except as expressly provided herein, no license or right is granted, by implication or otherwise, with respect to or under any patent application, patent, claims of patent or proprietary rights of Cargill. I shall not use any data or results obtained from the Facility to prepare or file any patent application in any country, or share any such data or results with any third party (including patent offices). No recording devices (e.g. video, camera, phone) may be used at the Facility without the prior approval of Cargill.

3. Assumption of Risk and Waiver: I assume all risks related to my access to the Facility. I waive and release Cargill (on my own behalf and that of my assigns, heirs and executors) from any and all claims, demands, damages, costs, expenses, rights and causes of action of whatever kind or nature (including, but not limited to, loss or injury to life, person or property) which may be sustained by me during, as a result of, or in any way connected with my access to the Facility.

4. Humane Handling of Livestock: Cargill is committed to handling livestock in a humane manner. Employees who work with and around livestock are given specific training in this area. Visitors and Vendors are not to come in contact with livestock in any form or fashion. If movement of livestock is needed or access to the livestock holding area is required, contact a Cargill management employee for assistance. When visiting a Facility where live animals are present, please immediately notify a Cargill representative if, within the previous ten (10) days, you have originated from or visited a country that has a known Foreign Animal Disease.

SCOPE: The requirements of this program shall apply to all persons entering into production and/or maintenance areas of the Facility, as well as persons entering into non-production areas.

AUTHORIZATION OF VISITORS AND VENDORS: All visitors and/or vendors entering into this Facility must be 18 years of age. Anyone under the age of 18 must have authorization by the applicable Department Manager prior to entry. All visitors entering into the Facility must have a Cargill employee escort. All visitors and/or vendors must sign in with security prior to entering the Facility and must sign out upon exiting.

PERSONAL SAFETY AND PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT: All visitors and/or vendors entering into any production and/or maintenance areas of the Facility shall wear personal protective equipment including but not limited to: hard-hat, heavy work boot, safety glasses, and hearing protection. Slips, trips, and falls are a great concern; therefore, footwear must have good soles for traction. Visitors entering production areas must be kept at least 3 feet away from machinery and from employees using knives, and shall be permitted access between boning tables only with proper authorization.

INJURY/ILLNESS REPORTING: All accidents, injuries, illnesses and/or conditions which may put product or personnel at risk involving visitors and/or vendors shall be reported to the Safety Department and/or Nurse's Department immediately. A written investigation of any accident and/or injury shall be submitted by the Cargill representative responsible for said person.

FOOD SAFETY: This facility operates under a Food Safety Management System. Any person entering into a production area of the Facility shall follow the Cargill Food Safety Program. Watches, rings, necklaces, bracelets, earrings, and any other jewelry shall be removed or not worn within the production areas unless otherwise authorized by Facility management. Frocks and hairnets shall be worn in production areas. Any tag that reads "HOLD" or "USDA or CIFA REJECTED" shall not be touched, moved, or disturbed in any way.

EATING AND SMOKING AREAS: Use of tobacco products is permitted only in designated areas. Food and beverage consumption is only allowed in designated areas. Spitting, smoking, chewing tobacco, chewing gum, or candy is strictly prohibited in production areas.

EMERGENCY OPERATING PLAN: In the event of an emergency, immediate and orderly evacuation of personnel is critical. If a fire, chemical release or other emergency is discovered, notify Facility management immediately. If an evacuation is ordered, all persons must evacuate the Facility.

PROCESS SAFETY MANAGEMENT (PSM): Use of Ammonia in the Cargill Facility refrigeration systems has proven to produce a minimal hazard as the system has been properly designed, installed, maintained and operated in a safe manner within OSHA guidelines.

POWERED INDUSTRIAL VEHICLES: Visitors shall not operate Powered Industrial Vehicles owned by Cargill.

MOTOR VEHICLE OPERATION: All visitors and/or vendors operating motor vehicles on the premises shall do so safely, shall not exceed 10 mph, and must wear seatbelts at all times on company premises.

HAZARD COMMUNICATION: Any and all chemicals brought into the Facility shall be properly labeled in accordance with OSHA regulations and authorized by the Safety Manager.

LOCKOUT/TAGOUT: Lockout/tagout is required any time individuals place their body in any point of operations. Never attempt to remove a lock from a piece of equipment. All persons using a lockout/tagout shall follow Cargill guidelines.

EHS MANAGEMENT SYSTEM: Cargill is committed to preventing injuries and pollution, complying with legal and other requirements, and continually improving EHS performance. All environmental inquiries must be forwarded to the Environmental Department. Contact the Safety Department for all safety inquiries.
